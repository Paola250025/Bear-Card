Ready, not scared 💛

A quick note before you build your kit.

Being prepared is not about fear. It is the opposite. When you know that a scraped knee, a sudden storm, a dead phone, or a power outage is already handled, you get to relax and actually enjoy the outdoors and everyday life.

You do not need to be a survival expert or spend a fortune. Most emergencies for regular families are small and common, and a few basics in one place solve almost all of them.

This kit walks you through exactly what to pack for home, car, and camping, plus a first-aid list, a family plan, and a contacts card. Check the boxes as you go. Build it in an afternoon.

🎯 The one habit that matters most: build one kit, keep it packed, and do not raid it for everyday use. A ready kit you never touch is a kit that is there when you need it.

⚠️ This is a beginner-friendly checklist, not medical or professional emergency training. Always follow local guidance, and in a real emergency, call your local emergency number.

The big picture

The 5 essentials 🧭

Do not memorize a huge list. Just think in these 5 simple buckets, everything else is a detail.

🩹

1. First aid

For the small stuff that happens most: cuts, scrapes, blisters, headaches.

💧

2. Water & warmth

The two things your body needs first: clean water and staying dry and warm.

🔦

3. Light & power

A way to see in the dark and keep your phone alive.

📻

4. Info & comms

A way to get news and reach help, plus your key contacts written down.

🧰

5. Tools & extras

The little items that fix problems: multi-tool, tape, cash, copies.

Home emergency kit 🏠

The grab-and-go basics for a power outage, storm, or evacuation.

    Water & food
  • Bottled water (aim for 1 gallon per person, per day, 3 days)
  • Non-perishable food for 3 days + a manual can opener
    • Light, power & info
  • Flashlight or headlamp + spare batteries
  • Power bank (charged) + charging cables
  • Battery or hand-crank radio
    • Health & comfort
  • First-aid kit (see the first-aid page)
  • One week of any needed medications
  • Blankets or emergency blankets, warm layers
  • Hygiene: wipes, hand sanitizer, toilet paper
    • Documents & extras
  • Copies of IDs, insurance, and this contacts card in a zip bag
  • Some cash in small bills
  • Whistle, multi-tool, duct tape, work gloves

Car emergency kit 🚗

For breakdowns, getting stuck, or being on the road when something happens.

    Car basics
  • Jumper cables or a jump-start pack
  • Tire pressure gauge + a way to inflate (or a sealant)
  • Reflective triangle or flares
  • Ice scraper (if you get cold weather)
    • Stay safe & warm
  • Flashlight or headlamp + spare batteries
  • Blanket + warm layers, hat and gloves
  • Bottled water + snacks that keep (bars, nuts)
  • Small first-aid kit
    • Handy extras
  • Phone power bank + cable
  • Multi-tool, duct tape, paper towels
  • Cash, and a printed copy of your contacts card

🅿️ Tip: keep a full water bottle and a blanket in the car year-round. They are the two things people most wish they had.

Camping emergency kit ⛺

The "just in case" items that turn a scary moment into a small one on the trail.

    Safety & navigation
  • First-aid kit + any personal meds
  • Headlamp + spare batteries
  • Whistle (3 blasts = help) + a small mirror
  • Paper map of the area + a compass
    • Warmth, water & fire
  • Emergency blanket or bivvy
  • Extra water + a way to purify (tablets or filter)
  • Waterproof matches or a lighter, in a dry bag
  • Rain jacket / extra dry layer
    • Fix-it & extras
  • Multi-tool or knife + duct tape
  • Extra snacks with calories
  • Charged phone + power bank (service can be spotty)
  • Tell someone your plan and return time before you go

First-aid kit 🩹

The basics that cover the small injuries families deal with most. Keep one at home, one in the car, one for camping.

    Wound care
  • Adhesive bandages, assorted sizes
  • Gauze pads + medical tape
  • Antiseptic wipes + antibiotic ointment
  • Blister bandages (a hiker's best friend)
  • Tweezers + small scissors
  • Disposable gloves
    • Medicine & comfort
  • Pain / fever reliever (adult + child, if needed)
  • Antihistamine for allergic reactions
  • Anti-itch cream + aloe for burns/bites
  • Any personal prescriptions
  • Elastic bandage for sprains + instant cold pack
  • A small card with allergies & emergency contacts

Don't set it and forget it

Keep your kit fresh 🔄

A kit only helps if it still works when you need it. Quick checks, done a few times a year.

How oftenCheck this
Every 3 monthsBatteries work, power bank is charged, flashlight turns on
Every 6 monthsRotate water and snacks; check for anything expired
Every 6 monthsUpdate contacts & medical info if anything changed
Once a yearRe-check clothing sizes for kids; refresh first-aid supplies
Before each tripGrab the camping kit, top off water, confirm the plan

📅 Easy trick: tie your checks to the clocks changing or the seasons, so you never have to remember a date.

Build-your-kit shopping list 🛒

Take this to the store (or order online). Most of it is cheaper than you think, and you may already own half.

  • Bottled water
  • Non-perishable food
  • Manual can opener
  • Flashlight / headlamp
  • Batteries (assorted)
  • Power bank + cables
  • Radio (battery/crank)
  • First-aid supplies
  • Emergency blankets
  • Whistle
  • Multi-tool / knife
  • Duct tape
  • Work gloves
  • Wipes / sanitizer
  • Water purification
  • Matches / lighter
  • Zip bags (waterproofing)
  • Small cash
  • Storage bin / backpack
  • Copies of documents

💡 Start with one kit. You do not need everything at once. Build the home kit first, then add the car and camping ones over time.

You're ready

That's it, you're ready 💛

No fear. Just a calm, capable family that has it handled.

You do not have to build the perfect kit today. You just have to start, one box checked, one item packed. A half-built kit is already better than none, and you can add to it over time.

Once your kit is ready, tuck it away and enjoy your adventures (and your everyday life) knowing that if something small goes sideways, you have got it.

🌲 Remember: it does not have to be perfect, it just has to happen. Being ready is a gift you give your future self, and the people you love.

⚠️ Safety note: This kit is a beginner-friendly guide, not medical or professional emergency training. Customize it for your family's needs, follow local guidance, and in a real emergency, always call your local emergency number.
