Bonus

5 Easy Campfire Meals

Kid-approved

Foil-Packet Fajitas

Sliced chicken or beef + peppers + onion + taco seasoning. Wrap in foil, cook on coals 15-20 min. Serve in tortillas.

Walking Tacos

Crush a bag of chips, add warm seasoned beef, cheese, lettuce & salsa right in the bag. No dishes!

Campfire Nachos

Chips + beans + cheese in a foil tray. Cover & heat by the fire until melty. Top with whatever you've got.

Banana Boats

Slit a banana (in peel), stuff with chocolate & mini marshmallows, wrap in foil, warm 5 min. Dessert!

Breakfast Burritos

Scramble eggs + cheese + pre-cooked sausage, roll in a tortilla. Make ahead & reheat in foil.

Classic S'mores

Graham cracker + chocolate + toasted marshmallow. The one everyone remembers. 🍫

Prep tip: chop and season everything at home in labeled bags, camp cooking becomes 10 minutes.

10 · Real Talk

What Nobody Tells You

The stuff I learned the hard way, so you don't have to

Arrive with daylight

Get there 2+ hours before dark. Setting up a tent in the dark with tired kids is a special kind of chaos.

One bin per category

Kitchen in one bin, sleep in another, first-aid in another. No digging through bags at night.

Freeze your water bottles

They keep the cooler cold AND become drinking water as they melt. Two jobs, one bottle.

Baby wipes are the MVP

Hands, faces, spills, quick "showers," even dishes. Bring double what you think.

A doormat saves your tent

A cheap mat at the tent door cuts the dirt inside in half. Shoes off, always.

Pack extra socks

Wet socks ruin moods faster than anything. Bring way more than seems reasonable.

Give each kid a flashlight

It becomes their prized possession, and it keeps them close after dark.

Familiar pillow = better sleep

A pillow and small night-light from home help little ones sleep in a new place.

11 · Step by Step

Set Up Camp in Order

Do it in this order and setup is calm, not chaos
  • Pick your spot. Flat ground, not under dead branches, slightly high (water runs downhill if it rains).
  • Lay the ground cover / tarp first. It protects the tent floor and keeps it dry.
  • Pitch the tent while you have daylight and helpers. This is the big one, do it first.
  • Make the beds now. Sleeping bags, pads, and pillows in before it gets dark.
  • Set up the kitchen zone. Away from the tent, on a flat surface.
  • Store all food in the car or cooler. Never in the tent (bears and ants).
  • Gather firewood before sunset. Hunting for wood in the dark is no fun.
  • Set out chairs and the lantern. Now sit down, you earned it.

12 · Plan B

When Things Go Sideways

Because something always does, here's what to do

It starts raining

Move activities under a tarp or into the car. Play cards, do the kids' pack, tell stories. Dry clothes ready for bedtime.

A kid won't sleep

Familiar pillow, night-light, and a calm routine. A little white noise (a fan app) covers scary night sounds.

Someone's cold at night

Hat on, dry socks, and a layer between them and the ground. Cold comes from below, not just the air.

Too many bugs

Bug spray at dusk, long sleeves, and keep food sealed. A citronella candle helps at the table.

You forgot something

Breathe. Most things you can improvise or buy at the camp store / nearest town. It's rarely a trip-ender.

A kid is scared

Name it, normalize it, give them a "job" (hold the flashlight). Confidence beats fear every time.
