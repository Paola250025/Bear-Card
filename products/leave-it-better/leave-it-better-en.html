Welcome, camp leader 👋

A quick note before your trip, then the kids take over.

Kids do not learn to respect nature from a lecture. They learn it from doing, from small missions that feel like a game and make them feel proud. That is exactly what this kit is.

Inside you will find two things working together: simple ground rules for the whole family (noise, neighbors, clean-up, wildlife), and games and badges that turn your kids into "Junior Rangers" who actually want to pick up litter and keep the campsite calm.

The Good Neighbor Code 🏕️

A campsite is a shared space. These are the unwritten rules that make you the family everyone is happy to camp next to.

🔇

Respect quiet hours

Most campgrounds have quiet hours (often 10pm to 7am). Voices, laughter, and car doors carry far at night. Keep it low after dark.

🎵

Music stays at your site

If your neighbor can hear your speaker, it is too loud. Many people camp for the quiet of nature. Use low volume, or headphones.

🚶

Do not cut through other sites

A campsite is someone's temporary home. Walk the roads and paths, do not shortcut through a neighbor's space, even if it is faster.

💡

Point lights down

Bright lanterns and headlamps blind everyone around you. Aim lights at the ground and dim them at night so everyone can see the stars.

🐕

Keep pets leashed and quiet

Leash your dog, clean up after it, and do not let it bark all night. Not everyone loves your pet as much as you do, and that is okay.

🔥

Never leave a fire alone

An unattended fire is dangerous for the whole campground. Drown it, stir it, and drown it again until it is cold before bed or leaving.

The big idea

Leave No Trace 🌎

Seven simple ideas that mean one thing: leave nature exactly as you found it, or better. Read these with your kids.

1

Plan ahead

Know the rules of where you are going and pack what you need so you are not caught off guard.

2

Stay on the trail

Walk and camp on marked paths and spots so plants and soil stay healthy.

3

Pack out your trash

Everything you bring in, you take out. If you packed it, you carry it home, even tiny wrappers.

4

Leave what you find

Rocks, flowers, sticks, and bugs belong to nature. Look, enjoy, take a photo, leave it there.

5

Be careful with fire

Only in fire rings, keep it small, and make sure it is fully cold before you walk away.

6

Respect wildlife

Watch animals from far away. Never feed them, human food makes them sick and unsafe.

🌟 The 7th rule: be kind to other people. Share the trail, keep the noise down, and smile. The outdoors belongs to everyone.

Game 1 · for the kids

Trash Treasure Hunt ♻️

A scavenger hunt, but backwards! Instead of taking things FROM nature, you find trash and take it OUT. Use gloves or a bag. Check a box for each thing you find and throw away the right way.

  • A candy or snack wrapper
  • A plastic bottle or can
  • A piece of paper or cardboard
  • A bottle cap or bread tie
  • A piece of plastic bag
  • Something that does not belong in nature
  • A tiny piece of litter most people would miss
  • BONUS: fill a whole bag with your family!

🏆 You win when: you leave your campsite cleaner than you found it. Found 5 or more? You just earned your Clean-Up Hero badge!

Game 3 · for the family

Spot & Fix the Campsite 🔍

Read each little story together. Ask your kids: what is wrong here, and how would a Junior Ranger fix it? Answers are underneath.

🎵 A family is playing loud music at 10pm and the campers next door cannot sleep.

Fix: Turn the music down or off after quiet hours. Nature and neighbors need rest too.

🍫 Someone dropped a candy wrapper on the trail and kept walking.

Fix: Pick it up and pack it out. A real Ranger even grabs trash that is not theirs.

🦝 A camper is leaving food out to "make friends" with a raccoon.

Fix: Never feed wild animals. Human food makes them sick. Store food away and watch from afar.

🌼 A kid picked a big handful of wildflowers to take home.

Fix: Leave what you find. Take a photo instead so the next family can enjoy them too.

🔥 A family is packing the car with the campfire still smoking.

Fix: Drown it, stir it, and drown it again until it is cold. Never leave a fire alone.

Mission · for the kids

The Quiet Camper Mission 🤫

Sound travels far in nature, especially at night. Learn your "outdoor voice levels" and earn your Quiet Camper badge.

0

Nature voice

Silent. For watching animals and listening to the forest.

1

Whisper

Only the person next to you can hear. Perfect after dark.

2

Inside voice

Calm and low. Good for talking around the campfire.

3

Big voice

Save it for open trails and daytime play, away from other camps.

🌙 Your mission: after the sun goes down, keep your voice at level 1 or 2 for the whole night. Do it and color in your Quiet Camper badge!

Before you leave · for the family

Leave It Better Checklist ✅

The most important pages! Before you drive away, walk the site together and check every box. This is how you leave it better than you found it.

  • All trash picked up and packed out (look under the table!)
  • Fire is drowned, stirred, and completely COLD to the touch
  • No food scraps or crumbs left on the ground
  • Tent spot cleared, nothing left behind
  • Rocks, logs, and sticks put back where we found them
  • Did a final "sweep" walk looking for tiny litter
  • Picked up at least one piece of trash that was not ours
  • Said thank you to the forest 🌲

👀 Ranger challenge: can a stranger tell your family camped here? If the answer is no, you did it perfectly.

Quick-Reference Card 📋

The whole kit in one glance. Snap a photo or print and clip it where you can see it.

Good neighbor basics

  • Quiet hours after dark (usually 10pm–7am)
  • Music low enough to stay at your own site
  • Lights pointed down, dimmed at night
  • Never cut through another family's site
  • Pets leashed, cleaned up after, and quiet

  • Pack out everything you packed in
