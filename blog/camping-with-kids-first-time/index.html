Camping With Kids for the First Time (Without Losing Your Mind)

By Paola · 7 min read
📌 Save to Pinterest

If the idea of your first family camping trip makes you a little nervous, you are completely normal, and you are also going to be fine. The nerves do not mean you are not ready. They mean you care. Here is how to plan a first trip that feels calm and doable, even if you have never pitched a tent in your life.

I am Paola, a mom and outdoorswoman, and I promise you this: your kids will not remember whether the tent was perfect. They will remember roasting marshmallows, spotting a deer, and falling asleep to the sound of the forest. Let's get you there.

1. Start small and close to home

For a first trip, you do not need a remote backcountry adventure. Pick a family campground one to two hours away, ideally one with bathrooms and a camp host. Book one or two nights, not a week. A short, easy first trip builds everyone's confidence, and if something goes sideways, home is not far.

2. Plan the trip on paper before you pack

The number one thing that turns a stressful trip into an easy one is planning it out ahead of time, instead of trying to remember everything in your head at 6am on departure day. Write down:

  • Your packing list (shelter, sleep, clothes, kitchen, first aid, fun)
  • A simple meal plan for each day, plus snacks
  • The campground rules, check-in time, and quiet hours
  • An emergency card: nearest ranger, hospital, and your campsite number

This is exactly why I built a done-for-you planner, so you are not starting from a blank page (more on that below).

3. Pack for comfort, not for "roughing it"

A first trip is not the time to be a minimalist. Warm sleep gear and good food make or break the experience for kids. Prioritize:

  • Warm sleeping bags and a sleeping pad (the ground is colder than you think)
  • Extra layers and dry socks, plus rain jackets just in case
  • Easy, familiar food, plus one fun treat like s'mores
  • A headlamp for each kid, they love having their own light

4. Give the kids a job (and something to do)

Bored kids at a campsite find trouble. Kids with a mission become your best helpers. Let them help set up the tent, collect (safe) sticks, or be in charge of the "bug patrol." Bringing along screen-free activities, a scavenger hunt, a nature journal, simple games, is the difference between "I'm bored" and "this is the best day ever."

5. Set up camp in the right order

When you arrive, resist the urge to unpack everything at once. Do it in order: tent first (while it is light), then sleeping gear, then the kitchen and food storage, and finally the fun stuff. Getting the "home base" ready first means that if the weather turns or the sun sets fast, you are covered.

6. Keep the first night simple

Do not over-schedule. A first night that is just dinner, a small campfire, and an early bedtime is a win. If everyone is warm, fed, and safe, you did it. Remember my favorite rule: it does not have to be perfect, it just has to happen.

What if my kid wants to go home?

Totally normal, especially the first night. Usually it passes once they are cozy in the sleeping bag. Bring a familiar comfort item, keep your own energy calm, and remember you can always leave early if you truly need to, though almost nobody does.

Your first family camping trip is not about doing it perfectly. It is about starting. And once you do, you will wonder why you waited so long. 🌲
