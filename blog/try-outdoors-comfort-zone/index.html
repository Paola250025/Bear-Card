Nervous to Try Camping, Hiking, or Archery? Start Anyway

By Paola · 5 min read

Everyone who loves the outdoors was once a total beginner who felt a little silly and a little scared. The only difference between them and the people still "waiting until they're ready" is simple: they started anyway.

I am Paola, and I still remember how intimidating it felt to try new outdoor things, wondering if I was doing it wrong, if I had the right gear, if people would notice I had no idea what I was doing. Here is what I learned: that feeling is not a stop sign. It is just the doorway.

It doesn't have to be perfect. It just has to happen.

Why "out of your comfort zone" feels so big

New things feel scary because your brain is trying to protect you from looking silly or failing. That is normal. But the outdoors is one of the most forgiving places to be a beginner, the trees do not judge your form, and the trail does not care if your backpack is fancy. The discomfort is not a sign you are not ready. It is a sign you are growing.

The trick: make the first step tiny

You do not have to summit a mountain or camp for a week. You just have to take a step so small it feels almost easy:

  • Camping? Book one night at a campground an hour from home.
  • Hiking? Pick a short, well-marked trail and just walk the first mile.
  • Archery? Learn the safety basics and shoot your first few arrows, no experience needed.

Small starts still count. In fact, they count the most, because they are the ones you will actually do.

You will do it "wrong," and that is the point

Your first tent setup will be crooked. Your first hike might be slower than you hoped. Your first arrows will fly funny. Good. That is what beginning looks like for everyone. The people who get good at things are simply the people who were willing to be bad at them first.

3 ways to make starting easier

  • Bring a friend or your family. Nervous is easier together, and it becomes a memory instead of a chore.
  • Lower the bar on purpose. The goal of your first time is not to do it well. It is just to do it.
  • Have a little bit of a plan. Knowing the basics ahead of time turns "scary unknown" into "okay, I've got this."

The version of you that already does this

Somewhere out there is the version of you who camps, who hikes, who shoots a bow, who feels at home outside. She is not a different person. She is just you, on the other side of starting.

So do not wait until you feel ready, because "ready" tends to never quite arrive. Pick the smallest possible first step, and take it this month. It does not have to be perfect. It just has to happen. 🌲
